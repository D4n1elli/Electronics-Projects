# Simple Flashlight 🔦

## How it works
The energy goes from the positive (+) side of the battery, traveling through the wire on the back and into the button. If the button isn't pressed, the energy stops there and nothing happens. Else, if it is pressed, the 3v go through the button and to the resistor, then to the LED positive side, lighting it up. The energy then goes to the negative (-) side of the LED, going to another wire that is connected to the negative side of the battery, closing the circuit. 


## Components
- 1 LED
- 2 wires
- Push-button
- 3v battery
- Soldering iron and solder
- Tape (can be replaced by anything that can hold the wires to the battery)
- Silicone glue (optional)


## How I made it
First I soldered both LED pins to a resistor (+ side) and to a wire (- side), then soldered the resistor to the button and taped the wire to the negative side of the battery.
Then, I soldered another wire to the other side of the button and taped it on the positive side of the battery.

Last, I used silicone glue to attach the circuit to a wooden stick so it is easier to hold and make the components safe from breaking.


## What I practiced
- Soldering
- Creating circuits
- Using Tinkercad to test the circuit before soldering it

## Version 2 ideas:
